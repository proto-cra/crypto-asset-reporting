What the Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework is

Overview

The Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework is [brief plain language explanation]. These [rules] impact crypto-asset users and other individuals and entities that provide business services facilitating exchange transactions in crypto-assets, referred to as crypto-asset service providers. This includes:

  • Crypto exchanges
  • Crypto-asset brokers and dealers
  • Operators of crypto-asset automated teller machines

Reporting crypto-asset service providers must collect detailed information on reportable users. They must verify and report this information to the CRA annually.

Source of the new rules

The bill containing the rules received Royal Assent on date TBD.

The rules are contained in Part XXI of the Income Tax Act and are based on the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development’s Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework.

What is a reporting crypto-asset service provider

Under the Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework, a reporting crypto-asset service provider is any individual or entity that, as a business, provides a service facilitating exchange transactions for or on behalf of customers. This service may include acting as a counterparty or as an intermediary to the exchange transactions or providing a trading platform.

What is a relevant crypto-asset

A relevant crypto-asset is any crypto-asset except:

  • Central bank digital currency
  • Specified electronic money products
  • Crypto-assets for which the RCASP has adequately determined that it cannot be used for payment or investment purposes
  • A crypto-asset that meets prescribed conditions

What is a relevant transaction

A relevant transaction is an exchange transaction or a transfer of a relevant crypto-asset.
