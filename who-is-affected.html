Who is affected

On this page

  • Reportable users
  • Reporting crypto-asset service providers

Reportable users

If you conduct at least one relevant transaction, you may be affected by the rules.

When the rules affect you

The rules affect you if you are a reportable user. A reportable user is a crypto-asset user that is a reportable person.

What is a crypto-asset user

A crypto-asset user is one of the following:

  • An individual or entity that is a customer of a reporting crypto-asset service provider for the purpose of carrying out relevant transactions, other than an individual or entity that meets both of the following conditions:
    • Is not a financial institution or a reporting crypto-asset service provider
    • Is acting as a customer of a reporting crypto-asset service provider for the purpose of carrying out relevant transactions for the benefit of, or on behalf of, another individual or entity as agent, custodian, nominee, signatory, investment advisor or intermediary
  • The individual or entity referred to in bullet B above, for whose benefit, or on whose behalf, relevant transactions are carried out
  • The customer that is the counterparty to the merchant for a reportable retail payment transaction, if both of the following are true:
    • A reporting crypto-asset service provider provides a service effectuating reportable retail payment transactions for or on behalf of the merchant
    • The reporting crypto-asset service provider is required to verify the identity of the customer under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act

What is a reportable person

A reportable person is either:

  • A natural person or an entity, other than an excluded person, that is resident in a reportable jurisdiction under the tax laws of that jurisdiction
  • An estate of an individual who was a resident of a reportable jurisdiction under the tax laws of that jurisdiction immediately before death

For the purpose of determining a reportable person, an entity that has no residence for tax purposes is deemed to be resident in the jurisdiction in which its place of effective management is situated.

What is a reportable jurisdiction

A reportable jurisdiction is any jurisdiction, including Canada.

For more information about residency, refer to:

  • Residency of a corporation
  • Residency of an individual
  • Residence of a trust or estate
When the rules don't affect you

The rules do not affect you if either:

  • You are an excluded person
  • You hold crypto-assets but have not conducted relevant transactions during the calendar year being reported

What is an excluded person

An excluded person is one of the following:

  • An entity, the stock of which is regularly traded on one or more established securities market
  • An entity that is a related entity of an entity described in bullet A above
  • A governmental entity
  • An international organization
  • A central bank
  • A financial institution, other than an investment entity described in bullet B in the definition of investment entity below:
    What is a financial institution

    A financial institution is a custodial institution, a depository institution, an investment entity or a specified insurance company.

    What is an investment entity

    An investment entity is an entity, other than an entity that is an active entity, that either:

    • Primarily carries on as a business one or more of the following activities or operations for or on behalf of a customer:
      • Trading in money market instruments (such as cheques, bills, certificates of deposit and derivatives), foreign exchange, transferable securities, commodity futures or exchange, interest rate and index instruments
      • Individual and collective portfolio management
      • Otherwise investing, administering or managing financial assets or relevant crypto-assets, money, or relevant crypto-assets on behalf of other persons, excluding through the provision of services effectuating exchange transactions for or on behalf of customers
    • The gross income of which is primarily attributable to investing, reinvesting or trading in financial assets, if the entity is managed by another entity that is a depository institution, a custodial institution, a specified insurance company or an entity described in bullet A above

Reporting crypto-asset service providers

If you facilitate exchange transactions as part of our business services, you may be affected by the rules.

When the rules affect you

The rules affect you if you are a reporting crypto-asset service provider (RCASP).

What is a reporting crypto-asset service provider (RCASP)

Under the CARF, a reporting crypto-asset service provider is any individual or entity that, as a business, provides a service facilitating exchange transactions for or on behalf of customers. This service may include acting as a counterparty or as an intermediary to the exchange transactions or providing a trading platform.

What is an exchange transaction

An exchange transaction is an exchange between relevant crypto-assets and fiat currencies or an exchange between one or more forms of relevant crypto-assets.

An RCASP has reporting obligations directly to the CRA for a calendar year if it is any of the following:

  • An entity or individual resident in Canada
  • An entity that is organized under the laws of Canada or a province and has an obligation to file tax returns or information returns in Canada
  • A partnership managed from Canada
  • An entity or individual that carries on a business in Canada

Reporting obligations apply to relevant transactions that are facilitated through a branch in Canada.

When the rules don't affect you

The rules do not affect you as an RCASP if equivalent reporting obligations and due diligence requirements are completed in a partner jurisdiction due to one of the following situations:

  • An RCASP is resident in a partner jurisdiction
  • An RCASP is incorporated and organized under the laws of a partner jurisdiction and has either:
    • Legal personality in a partner jurisdiction
    • An obligation to file tax returns or information returns to the tax authorities in the partner jurisdiction with respect to the income of the entity
  • An RCASP is being managed from the partner jurisdiction
  • An RCASP facilitates relevant transactions through a branch in a partner jurisdiction

In cases where an entity meets the definition of an RCASP but has completed their reporting obligations and due diligence requirements in a partner jurisdiction based on the situations specified above, the RCASP must notify us by filing a Part XXI Information Return indicating so.

For more information, please refer to Chapter X of the filing instructions.
